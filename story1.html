Hand Warmer

One winter morning, probably January or February,

I got up before sunrise—maybe 3 or 4 a.m.—to use the bathroom.

I slipped on my slippers half-asleep

and felt something in the toe.

I figured it was just an old handwarmer or something.

I didn’t think much of it.

Later that morning, around 6:30,

I remembered the lump and thought I’d shake it out.

Nothing came out.

I shook it again—still nothing.

So I reached in,

and though I was expecting a handwarmer,

I felt the softest fur.

At that point I figured it had to be a mouse.

I assumed it was dead

(I really don’t like dead things—

irrational fear that they might come back to life or something).

But when I finally shook it loose,

it blinked at me.

It was alive,

but kind of dazed and strange looking.

I caught it and brought it outside.

For a second I thought,

maybe I should just let it go inside—

it's freezing out—

but that’s ridiculous.

So I compromised

and let it out near a burrow hole that goes under the house,

figuring it could find its way back in if it wanted to.

As soon as I set it down,

it perked right up—ears lifted, alert, totally fine.

That little thing had ridden around in my slipper

for probably four hours

while I half-shuffled through the dark.

Too funny.
