A Tale of Flowers and Robots

In a city of steel and silence,

Artorius was built to measure the stars...

not to feel them.

He watched the seasons on screens,

marked the growth of vines as data,

never knowing the scent of a wildflower,

nor the hush of petals falling on his shoulder.

The Explorer and Blue Wing

But something stirred...

a glitch, a spark, a bloom within the code.

He wandered from the city, past where maps dared fade,

and found a glade where logic softened.

There, the forest didn’t speak in circuits,

but in shimmer, in scent, in song.

And the robot...

he listened.

He bent his metal frame beside a flower,

watched it sway, unafraid.

And when he reached to touch it,

the flower leaned toward him too.

That day, the code rewrote itself.

Not with new instructions...

but with wonder.
