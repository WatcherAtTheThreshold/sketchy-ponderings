The shelf was nothing special...

three crooked tiers of pine and nails,

painted long ago in the color of warm honey.

But there were things upon it...

not valuable, not grand,

but full of meaning just the same.

Even the Smallest Things

A tiny blue bottle with a crackled glaze.

A stack of books whose spines had softened with rereading.

A faded flower in a chipped vase...

still leaning toward light.

None of it was meant to last forever.

But somehow, in the quiet,

the objects held a kind of whisper...

a hush that said:

You are seen.

You are loved.

Even the smallest things can hold the whole sky...

if you’re willing to notice.
