It leans slightly, like it’s tired from watching the wind.

A tree in the marsh, neither tall nor proud...

but still standing.

Yellow Landscape

They call it the Listening Tree.

Not because it speaks,

but because it never interrupts.

People come when their hearts are too loud.

They sit in the moss. Say nothing. Or everything.

And the tree receives it all...

not to remember, but to hold.

The wind brushes its leaves like breath.

The fog gathers at its roots like prayer.

And still it listens...

for the grief that has no words,

and the joy too sacred to name.
